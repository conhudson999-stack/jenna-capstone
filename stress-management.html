Stress Management & Mindfulness

Stress is a natural reaction that our body has when changes, pressure, or difficult situations occur. Stress is normal and is experienced by everyone at some point. We all experience and cope with stress differently based on different situations.

Too much stress may feel like: difficulty with concentration, lack of motivation, feeling overwhelmed, irritability, and anxiety.

Stress Management Methods: Deep-Breathing, Journaling, Rest, Positive Self-Talk, Visualization/Meditation, Progressive Muscle Relaxation, Peer Support, Music, Organizing Tasks, Self-Care, and Hobbies.

Mindfulness Approach, KAWA Model: KAWA means "River" in Japanese.

  • Rocks = Challenges you have faced
  • Riverbank = Your environmental supports around you
  • Driftwood = Personal Strengths/Assets you have that bring you through each challenge
  • Water = Represents yourself moving through your sport season, life, etc.

Pre-Game Strategies:

  • 3 consecutive Deep-Breathing Exercises: "Box-Breathing Method"
  • Develop a Mantra: short, positive phrases or words to set the tone for your game/practice
  • Visualization: imagine and picture successful execution of a game or specific play. Use all of your senses to create the real environment. Practice it frequently prior to the actual game.

We all experience stress in different ways. Explore different stress management strategies that work for you in your daily life and in relation to your sport.
