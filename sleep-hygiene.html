Sleep

Sleep/rest is an activity that supports active engagement and participation in your daily life. It is the primary time for mental and physical restoration related to replenishing energy, while reducing inflammation and stress.

Young adults need over 8 hours of sleep per night to function their best physically, emotionally, and cognitively.

During sleep, the following occur: muscle building/body repair, immune system boosting, brain debris clearing/memory consolidation, and emotion resetting.

Sleep & Physical Health: Poor sleep is associated with delayed response/reaction time, fatigue, and poor decision-making. Athletes who are sleep deprived are at an increased risk for injury and delayed recovery following injuries. Muscle recovery happens during sleep.

Sleep & Mental Health: Sleep deprivation can lead to irritability. The REM stage of sleep plays a role in how we process our emotions and mood.

Sleep & Cognition/Academics: Memory consolidation occurs during REM sleep, organizing what new learning to keep and what should be deleted. Sleep deprivation impairs cognitive performance and has been linked to lower grades.

Sleep Recommendations:

  1. Try to maintain a regular sleep schedule every day of the week. While this is not always possible, you can establish a night routine that can be followed regularly.
  2. Try to avoid bright, blue light before bed because it can delay your ability to fall asleep.
  3. Try to avoid caffeine at least 6 hours before bed.
  4. If you feel sleep-deprived, short-duration naps throughout the day can be beneficial. However, if you typically have trouble falling asleep, you may want to avoid naps.
  5. Try to avoid completing homework/studying while sitting in your bed.

Relaxation Methods for Sleep: Deep-breathing (Box Breathing Method, Diaphragmatic Breathing), Meditation (self-led or using a guided video), and Journaling (gratitude journaling/brain-dump your day). These can help us to reset our nervous systems and work out of that "fight or flight" state.

Primary Recovery Methods: Sleep/Rest and Nutrition/Hydration.

Additional Recovery Methods:

  • Cryotherapy, including ice/ice baths
  • Manual Therapy, including foam rolling, massage, and compression
  • Stretching, including static, dynamic, and bands
  • Low-Intensity Exercise, including walking and cycling

Key Takeaways

  • Sleep is a key area of recovery that we should prioritize and aim for consistency.
  • Explore different strategies to promote increased sleep and keep a sleep log to find what works best for you.
  • Young adults need over 8 hours of sleep per night to function their best physically, emotionally, and cognitively.
