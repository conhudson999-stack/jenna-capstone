Time Management

Time management can impact all areas of our lives, including academics, athletics, productivity, and performance. Prioritizing time management can help promote areas of both physical and mental health.

Step 1: Block out high priority and fixed demands such as classes, practices/games, and appointments.

Step 2: Add other important tasks around your high priority tasks, such as meals, rest/sleep, homework/studying, recovery, self-care/leisure, and social participation.

For high priority tasks that are not fixed, try to prioritize them earlier in the day when your energy is the highest — for example, homework/studying. Try to organize your tasks in a way that prevents overexertion or overload in your day or week. Remember to fuel your body and take breaks for yourself when you need it to replenish your energy throughout the day.

Use prioritization to determine what tasks have higher priority — for example, meals, sleep, and homework. Adapt this to your own needs.

Planning, prioritization, and time management may promote increased mental energy and physical health so that you can prevent burnout and fatigue throughout the semester or season.

Key Takeaways

  • Time management can impact academics, athletics, productivity, and performance.
  • Planning your day ahead can have many benefits.
  • Explore and adapt a calendar, planner, or checklist to what you find is most beneficial for you.
